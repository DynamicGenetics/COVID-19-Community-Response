User guide


This is a short guide to the dashboard and how to use it to identify vulnerable communities in Wales

A full guide can be found in the Wiki

Scroll down or press the button to get started


About


This dashboard is an opportunity to explore data from government, community and social media sources to better understand the distribution of community support and community-level vulnerabilities in Wales during the COVID-19 pandemic. You can choose the variables you want to compare to answer the questions that you are interested in.

Our data


We have combined NHS and census data with online sources such as Twitter and Police Rewired. Most of the data used for the map is publicly available. You can access our data on our GitHub page or view our data documentation on the Wiki guide. Some data is not publicly available, but we always report our sources and the results from this data can still be viewed in the dashboard.

We kindly thank the WCVA, Mutual Aid, Welsh Government Statistics and Research for collecting and making this data available. We also thank Open Street Map for their map of Wales, you can suggest improvements here.

Data layers


There are two types of data layers:

  • Local need: Factors which indicate that a community may be at elevated risk during the COVID-19 pandemic

  • Local support: Factors which indicate that a community may have better resources for coping during COVID-19 pandemic

We are using covid mutual aid database to identify community support groups, if you don't your local group on the map you can register it here.


Automatic analysis


As you select layers, analyses will be made to determine which areas are most at risk in terms of the selected factors. Selecting two layers will initiate a bivariate analysis to identify vulnerable areas in terms of both factors.

Note: Your view will reset each time you reload the map.
