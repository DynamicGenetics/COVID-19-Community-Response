User guide


This is a short guide to the dashboard and how to use it to identify vulnerable communities in Wales

A full guide can be found in the Wiki

Press the icons or scroll down to get started


About


This dashboard is an opportunity to explore data from government, community and social media sources to better understand the distribution of community support and community-level vulnerabilities in Wales during the COVID-19 pandemic. You can choose the variables you want to compare, to answer the questions that you are interested in.

Our data


We have combined NHS and census data with online sources such as Twitter and Police Rewired. Most of the data used for the map is publicly available (open data) and you can view or access our data here ( Wiki guide ). Some data is not publicly available but we always report our sources and the results from this data can still be viewed in the dashboard.

Data layers


There are two types of data layers:

  • Risk factors: Factors which indicate that a community may be at elevated risk during the COVID-19 pandemic

  • Community resources: Factors which indicate that a community may have better resources for coping during COVID-19 pandemic


Automatic analysis


As you select layers, analyses will be made to determine which areas are most at risk in terms of the selected factors. Selecting two layers will initiate a bivaraite analysis to identify vulnerable areas in terms of both factors.
